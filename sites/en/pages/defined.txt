The <b>defined</b> function in Perl checks if the given value is anything but <a href="/undef">undef</a>

=abstract end

<include file="examples/defined.pl">

<b>defined</b> means it is NOT <a href="/undef">undef</a>

<b>not defined</b> means it is <a href="/undef">undef</a>

Being "<a href="/false">false</a>" or "<a href="/true">true</a>" have different meaning than being "defined" or "not defined".
